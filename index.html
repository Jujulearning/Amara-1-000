Who Amara is for

1,000
days.

The 1,000 days between pregnancy and a child's second birthday set the foundation for all the days that follow. What happens in this window shapes a child's brain, their health, and their future — for life.

🌸 🌿 🌸
01
Building Brains

More brain development happens in the first 1,000 days than at any other time in life. Nutrition during pregnancy and infancy directly shapes cognitive development, memory, and learning capacity. Folate, iron, omega-3, and choline are not optional — they are foundational.

02
Building Health

The immune system, metabolic function, and organ development are all shaped during this window. What a mother eats during pregnancy and what a baby receives in their first two years determines risk for chronic disease decades later.

03
Building a Fair Start

Children who are well-nourished in the first 1,000 days are more likely to thrive in school, stay healthy, and break cycles of poverty. Nutrition in this window is one of the most powerful equity levers we have — and one of the most underfunded.

04
Building Prosperity

Every dollar invested in maternal and infant nutrition yields an estimated $16 in economic returns through improved health, productivity, and reduced healthcare costs. The 1,000 days are not just a health window — they are an economic one.

"The 1,000 days between a woman's pregnancy and her child's second birthday offer a unique window of opportunity to shape healthier and more prosperous futures."

— 1,000 Days Organization · thousanddays.org

How Amara began

The story that
started everything.

Juanita's sister was pregnant. She received nutrition advice from three places at the same time. Her nutritionist. Her community. Her mother. All three gave her different answers. None of them agreed. And no one reconciled them.

"No one told her which advice was evidence-based, which was tradition, and which was somewhere in between. She was left to figure it out alone."

She was overwhelmed — not because she didn't care, but because the system gave her no way to navigate it. The guidance she received didn't reflect her food, her culture, or her kitchen.

That confusion is not rare. It is the everyday reality for millions of mothers. And Amara was built to change it.

Full nutritional intelligence

We track everything. Not just the obvious.

Most apps track calories. Amara tracks your complete nutritional picture — macronutrients, key vitamins, minerals, and the cultural foods that matter most to you and your baby. When you log collard greens, Amara shows you the folate, calcium, iron, vitamin K, and fiber. Your food, fully seen.

Flora AI advisor

Available at 2am. No appointment. No judgment.

Flora is powered by Claude and trained to know your food. She suggests egusi seeds for iron, callaloo for folate, lentil dal for pregnancy support — not because she assumes, but because you told her your kitchen. She also monitors early warning signs of preeclampsia, postpartum depression, and gestational diabetes. Flora is an escalation assistant, not a diagnosis tool. Every response is an action: monitor, call your provider, or go to the ER.

🌸 🌿 🌸
Dual health tracker

Two plants. One garden. Both watched.

The mother has a plant. The baby has a plant. They grow side by side, and Amara watches both. WHO growth charts for the baby. Edinburgh PPD screening, blood pressure monitoring, and mood tracking for the mother. Because you cannot fully tend one without tending the other.

The team behind Amara

Built from two kitchens.
Two cultures. One conviction.

Amara was not built from a research paper. It was built from lived experience — and from a belief that a mother's nutritional plan should include the food she actually cooks.

Erica Stephens
MPH · Johns Hopkins University
Co-Founder

Erica grew up eating Black Southern food — collard greens slow-cooked with smoked turkey, black-eyed peas, sweet potato, butter beans. Her grandmother's kitchen was nutritious. No prenatal handout ever said so. She built Amara's technical foundation with one conviction: the tools we build should work for the communities most often left out of their design.

🌿 Black American Southern food traditions
Juanita Julius Ndyamukama
MSc · Johns Hopkins University
Co-Founder

Juanita grew up in Tanzania eating mchicha, ugali, and maharage — foods that have sustained East African mothers for generations. Her sister's experience with conflicting nutrition advice during pregnancy is what started Amara. Her nutrition expertise shaped every food in Amara's database. What the system called other, she knew as nourishing.
