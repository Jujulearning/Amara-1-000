We track everything.
Not just the obvious.

Most apps track calories. Amara tracks your complete nutritional picture — macronutrients, micronutrients, and the cultural foods that matter most to you and your baby.

🔥
Macronutrients

Total calories, carbohydrates, protein, and fats — tracked per meal and per day, benchmarked to pregnancy and postpartum requirements.

🫀
Iron

Blood volume doubles during pregnancy. Amara tracks iron daily and suggests culturally familiar iron-rich foods — not just spinach and red meat.

🧬
Folate & B12

Critical for neural tube development in the first trimester. Amara flags gaps and connects them to foods from your own cultural tradition.

🦴
Calcium

Essential for bone and teeth development. Tracked across dairy and non-dairy sources — including plant-based and cultural alternatives.

☀️
Vitamin D

Supports immune function and calcium absorption. Often deficient. Amara surfaces vitamin D gaps and connects them to practical food sources.

🐟
Omega-3

Essential for fetal brain and eye development. Amara tracks omega-3 across cultural food sources — including fish preparations common in non-Western cuisines.

💧
Hydration

Blood volume expands significantly during pregnancy. Daily water intake is tracked and adjusted for trimester and postpartum stage.

🌿
Cultural food library

A growing database of Black American Southern, West African, Caribbean, South Asian, Latin American, and East Asian foods — each with full nutritional profiles.

Food inclusivity is not a feature. It is the foundation.

Every food in Amara's database is mapped to its full nutritional profile. When a mother logs collard greens, she sees its folate, calcium, iron, vitamin K, and fiber — not a generic entry. That is what food inclusivity looks like.

Our cultural lens

The nutrition advice we received was not built for us.

Standard prenatal guidance centers Western foods. A mother whose kitchen centers jollof rice, dal, or callaloo receives advice that does not reflect her food — or validate it. Amara changes that.

"A friend was told by her OB: no sushi during pregnancy. But her mother had made her fish her whole childhood. Fish is an excellent source of omega-3 — essential for fetal brain development. The blanket ban created a conflict between medical advice and a lifetime of cultural knowledge."

— The story that started Amara

Platform features

Everything in one garden.

Six features built to work as a system — because a mother's health and her child's development are not separate outcomes.

🥗

Cultural nutrition tracking

Full macronutrient and micronutrient tracking across a growing library of culturally diverse foods. Log what you actually eat — Amara does the nutritional work.

🌿

Flora AI advisor

Powered by Claude. Culturally fluent, available 24/7. Flora answers nutrition questions with cultural specificity and flags early warning signs of preeclampsia, PPD, and gestational diabetes.

📈

Infant growth tracker

WHO-standard growth charts for weight, height, and head circumference. Visualize your baby's development over time against established percentile bands.

💜

Maternal health screening

Edinburgh PPD scale, blood pressure monitoring, mood tracking, and gestational diabetes indicators. The dual garden — both plants watched, always.

🌸

Milestone celebrations

First smile, first foods, first steps — tracked and celebrated. Each milestone makes the garden grow. Your 1,000-day journey, beautifully marked.

🔔

Daily reminders

Gentle daily nudges personalized to your gestational week or infant's age — hydration, vitamins, nutrition logs, and wellbeing check-ins.

Why it matters

The most critical window in human development.

What a baby is exposed to from conception through age two shapes their brain architecture, immune function, and metabolic health for life. Nutrition is one of the most powerful levers we have — and most tools waste it on generic, culturally narrow advice.

🌍
Culturally grounded

Every food suggestion, every Flora response, every reminder is shaped by the mother's cultural background — not a generic Western default. Food is culture. Amara honors that.

🛡️
Clinically responsible

Flora is an escalation assistant — not a diagnostic tool. Every output is an action: monitor at home, call your provider, or go to the ER now. Safety over scale, always.

💸
Free for mothers. Always.

Amara is free for the mothers who need it most. Revenue comes from health systems and payors — not users. This is non-negotiable.

🔒
Private by design

HIPAA-compliant from day one. End-to-end encryption. No data sold. No advertising. Mothers own their data and can delete it at any time.

The team

Built from lived experience.

Amara was not built from a research paper. It was built from two kitchens, two cultures, and a shared conviction that a mother's nutritional plan should include the food she actually cooks.

Erica Stephens
Co-Founder

Erica grew up eating Black Southern food — collard greens slow-cooked with smoked turkey, black-eyed peas on New Year's Day, sweet potato, butter beans. Juanita Julius Ndyamukama
Co-Founder

Juanita grew up in Tanzania eating mchicha, ugali, and maharage — foods that have sustained East African mothers for generations. When she arrived in the United States and saw pregnancy nutrition materials, she saw a system built for someone else. Her nutrition expertise shaped Amara's entire food database. What she knew as nourishing, the system called other. Amara is the answer to that.
